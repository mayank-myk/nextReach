5 Tips for Creating Viral Content with Influencers

Written by Sudheesh@NextReach.Ai

In today’s digital-first world, creating viral content for social media platforms is the holy grail for brands. Viral posts don’t just attract attention—they drive massive engagement, boost brand awareness, and can lead to skyrocketing sales. But what’s the secret to going viral? One key component is influencer marketing. Influencers’ creativity, relatability, and ability to connect with audiences make them the perfect partners for crafting share-worthy content.

Whether you’re launching a new product or building a brand presence, working with influencers can help your content reach unprecedented levels of visibility. By integrating innovative strategies like the pay-per-view (PPV) model, you can ensure your campaigns are not just creative but also cost-effective. Here are five proven tips for creating viral content with influencers.

1. Focus on Relatable and Authentic Content

Authenticity is king when it comes to content that resonates with audiences. Viral content often stems from relatable, real-life moments that strike a chord with viewers. According to Edelman’s Trust Barometer, 63% of consumers trust influencers more than traditional advertising.

When collaborating with influencers, encourage them to create content that reflects their real personality and voice rather than pushing heavily scripted promotions. Platforms like Instagram and TikTok thrive on authenticity, where even simple videos can amass millions of views if they resonate with audiences.

2. Leverage Trends to Spark Engagement

Social media trends are the fuel for viral content. Influencers are often at the forefront of these trends, whether it’s a new challenge, a catchy hashtag, or a viral meme. Partnering with influencers who understand and can tap into these trends allows your brand to ride the wave of what’s already capturing public attention.

For example, if a trending dance challenge aligns with your brand’s target audience, you could work with influencers to create branded content using the trend. Tools like Google Trends or platforms like Trendhunter can help identify emerging trends that fit your brand’s voice.

3. “Hook” the Viewer in the First 3-5 Seconds

The internet is a visual playground, and eye-catching content has the best chance of going viral. Influencers are masters at creating visually engaging posts. The first 3-5 seconds—or the first slide in a carousel—is key to grabbing attention.

Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube are perfect for sharing such visually impactful hook based content. For instance, a travel influencer might start a video with breathtaking drone footage of a scenic destination and then gently slide information about your brand’s service or product; or a food influencer might start the video with a stunning video of them consuming a tasty dish and then show a recipe video showcasing your brand's ingredients.

4. Nudge engagement from viewer

Viral campaigns often succeed because they invite viewers to like, save, share or comment. Challenges, giveaways, and hashtags can also motivate users to create and share their own content featuring your brand. Influencers can amplify these campaigns by initiating the trend and encouraging their followers to join in.

For example, a fitness brand could partner with an influencer to launch a 7-day fitness challenge. By using a branded hashtag, followers can share their own progress, creating a ripple effect that extends the campaign’s reach. This type of user-generated content (UGC) not only drives engagement but also builds a sense of community around your brand.

Pro Tip: Consider rewarding participants with exclusive offers or shoutouts to keep the momentum going. Influencers can help sustain engagement by showcasing the best submissions on their platforms.

5. Embrace the Pay-Per-View (PPV) Model for Precision Marketing

While creativity is essential for virality, ensuring your content reaches the right audience is equally important. The PPV influencer marketing model allows you to pay only for actual views, ensuring that your content is being seen by genuinely engaged audiences.

For example, if you collaborate with an influencer to create a hilarious product demo video, you can set a PPV campaign to target viewers who are likely to resonate with the humour and share the content. This precision helps brands scale their campaigns while maintaining cost-effectiveness.

Pro Tip: Combine PPV campaigns with retargeting ads in performance marketing to keep the momentum alive by reaching viewers who’ve already shown interest in your viral content.

Final Thoughts: The Recipe for Viral Success

Creating viral content with influencers is both an art and a science. By focusing on authenticity, tapping into trends, crafting visually engaging content, and encouraging participation, you can significantly boost your chances of going viral. The pay-per-view model adds a layer of accountability and efficiency, ensuring your investment drives meaningful engagement.
