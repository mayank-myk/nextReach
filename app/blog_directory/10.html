How Influencer Marketing Can Help Brands Reach Gen Z and Millennials

Written by Yash@NextReach.Ai

As Gen Z and Millennials become the dominant consumers, brands are racing to understand and connect with these highly profitable audiences. Together, these generations represent billions of dollars in purchasing power, making them crucial targets for businesses across all industries. Traditional marketing tactics, however, often fail to resonate with younger audiences who prioritise authenticity, transparency, and digital connection. This is where influencer marketing has become invaluable.

By collaborating with trusted influencers, brands can create relatable, engaging content that speaks to the values and lifestyles of Gen Z and Millennials. And now, with innovative models like pay-per-view (PPV) influencer marketing, brands have more precise tools to maximise ROI while engaging these powerful demographics. Here’s how influencer marketing can bridge the gap and drive meaningful engagement with Gen Z and Millennial consumers.

1. Understanding Gen Z and Millennials: Values and Preferences

To understand the effectiveness of influencer marketing for these generations, it’s essential to know what drives their engagement. According to Pew Research Center, Millennials (born 1981–1996) and Gen Z (born 1997–2012) both prioritise authenticity, social responsibility, and convenience. They are also highly digital-savvy, spending significant time on social media, streaming platforms, and mobile apps. Gen Z, in particular, is known for being socially conscious, seeking brands that align with their understanding of sustainability and inclusivity.

pewresearch.org

These generations respond to experiences over traditional advertising, making influencer marketing an ideal approach. By creating genuine, personalised, and niche-oriented content, brands can resonate with these audiences. Platforms like Think with Google have shown that Gen Z and Millennials value social proof and peer recommendations, especially from influencers they admire.

thinkwithgoogle.com

2. The Role of Authentic Content

In an era where consumers are overwhelmed with content, authenticity is a must-have for any brand hoping to distinguish themselves amongst the younger audiences. Forbes highlights that Gen Z and Millennials are highly sceptical of obvious advertising and gravitate toward brands that deliver unique, different and authentic experiences. Influencers play a crucial role in this by bridging the gap between brands and consumers with content that feels relatable and real.

For instance, when an influencer shares their personal experience with a product, it holds far more credibility than a typical advertisement. This authenticity factor is amplified through micro- and nano-influencers, who often have smaller, highly engaged communities. Their followers see them as friends and are more likely to trust their opinions.

forbes.com

With a PPV model, brands can further refine their targeting by investing in influencers who truly connect with their target audience, ensuring that content only reaches engaged viewers. This focus on quality over quantity aligns perfectly with Gen Z and Millennial values, as it prioritises genuine engagement over broad reach.

3. Leveraging Short-Form Video Content

Short-form video content, such as TikTok videos, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts, has become a powerful tool for capturing the attention of younger consumers. According to HubSpot, short-form videos have a high engagement rate, especially among Gen Z, who prefer bite-sized, entertaining, and informative content.

hubspot.com

Several top influencers have mastered the art of creating short, impactful videos that educate or entertain in an engaging way. For example, beauty influencers might create quick tutorials or product reviews, while travel influencers share immersive destination experiences. These brief videos pass information quickly to audiences which are then more likely to follow through on calls to action, such as visiting a brand’s website or making a purchase.

Brands utilising PPV influencer marketing can leverage these short-form videos in a cost-effective approach, paying only for actual views generated. This way, they know their investment is directly aligned with the content’s performance, particularly when reaching a demographic that favours short, engaging content.

4. Creating Community and Engagement Through Social Causes

Gen Z and Millennials are deeply connected to social causes and expect the brands they support to share these values. Influencers who advocate for causes like sustainability, mental health, and diversity can be instrumental in helping brands establish credibility in these areas. According to Nielsen, brands that engage with social causes can experience up to a 70% increase in brand favorability among younger audiences.

nielsen.com

When influencers openly discuss topics like eco-consciousness or inclusivity, they foster a sense of community with their followers. Brands that partner with these influencers can benefit from the loyalty and trust generated by these shared values. For example, a fashion brand collaborating with influencers who advocate for sustainable fashion not only aligns with Gen Z values but also establishes trust and long-term customer relationships.

5. Interactive and Experiential Content: The Key to Engagement

Interactive content—such as live-streaming, Q&A sessions, and “day-in-the-life” videos—has shown to significantly enhance engagement among Gen Z and Millennials. These audiences value two-way communication, and interactive content offers the perfect opportunity for brands to connect on a personal level. Platforms like TikTok and Instagram offer excellent engagement tools, including polls, quizzes, comment replies, and other live video features, which influencers can leverage to drive audience participation.

For brands using PPV influencer marketing, this interaction becomes even more powerful in terms of ROI. By partnering with influencers for exclusive live-streamed events or interactive sessions, brands can attract highly engaged viewers willing to invest time and attention in the experience. This approach also creates a sense of exclusivity, appealing to younger audiences who value unique and memorable interactions with their favourite influencers.

6. Leveraging the Pay-Per-View (PPV) Model for Cost-Effective Reach

The PPV model aligns perfectly with influencer marketing strategies targeting Gen Z and Millennials, as it allows brands to pay only for content that garners genuine interest. This model not only provides transparency but also ensures that marketing budgets are used efficiently by focusing on actual views. As PPV gains traction, it offers brands a solution to the challenge of measuring ROI in influencer campaigns.

For example, a skincare brand targeting Gen Z can partner with a popular skincare influencer, paying only for views on educational content about skincare routines. The PPV model ensures that every dollar spent reaches a truly engaged audience, improving campaign accountability. This targeted approach also offers value to influencers, as they can benefit financially by producing high-quality, high-engagement content that attracts more views.

Practical Tips for Brands Looking to Leverage Influencer Marketing with Gen Z and Millennials

  • Identify Relevant Influencers: Work with influencers who already have a following among Gen Z and Millennials and align with your brand values.
  • Prioritise Authentic Content: Encourage influencers to share personal experiences and insights about your products rather than scripted or overly produced content.
  • Focus on Short-Form Video: Invest in short-form video platforms like TikTok and Instagram Reels, as these formats are highly engaging for younger audiences.
  • Highlight Social Causes: Partner with influencers who advocate for issues like sustainability, inclusivity, or mental health to foster a sense of community with your audience.
  • Experiment with Interactive Content: Use live-streaming, polls, and Q&A sessions to make content more engaging and relatable to younger viewers.
  • Leverage the PPV Model: Utilise PPV influencer marketing to maximise budget efficiency and ensure your brand’s content reaches genuinely interested viewers.
